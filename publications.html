Key Papers

Selected manuscripts that define the core QDL framework and its gravitational, electromagnetic, and metrological consequences.

Quantized Dimensional Ledger as a Unified Dimensional Closure Framework for the Standard Model and Gravity

Bourassa, J. D. (2025). Zenodo. DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17742903

Flagship QDL paper embedding Standard Model fields and a scalar–tensor gravity sector into a common \(3L + 2F\) closure rule, using a Quantized Dimensional Cell to organize fields, couplings, and constants.

A Unified Ledger-Based Framework for Physical Theories: From Dimensional Cells to Scalar–Tensor Field Structure

Bourassa, J. (2025). Zenodo. DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17704896

Develops the QDC and a covariant scalar–tensor action with QDL-constrained couplings, establishing a route from ledger closure to field-theory structure and metrological interpretation.

The Quantized Dimensional Cell as an SO(3,2) Space–Time Structure

Bourassa, J. D. (2025). Zenodo. DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17683786

Identifies the QDC as a \(3L + 2F\) geometric object embedded in an SO(3,2)-like structure, linking ledger exponents to higher-dimensional space–time and conformal symmetries.

The Ledger Geometry of Magnetic Moments: A QDL Derivation of the Electron and Muon g–2 Factors

Bourassa, J. (2025). Zenodo. DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17693061

Reinterprets electron and muon anomalous magnetic moments in terms of ledger geometry, connecting loop corrections and g–2 factors to dimensional-flow structure within the QDL framework.

The Quantized Dimensional Ledger for Metrology: Dimensional Closure, QMU Ledgers, and the Ontology of Physical Constants

Bourassa, J. D. (2025). Zenodo. DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17619526

Introduces the Quantized Measurement Unit (QMU) ledger as a dimensional audit tool, classifying constants and tying metrology practice directly to the QDL closure principle.

QDL Experimental Validation Protocol: A Unified Roadmap for Torsion, NV-Center, Cavity, and Metamaterial Tests

Bourassa, J. (2025). Zenodo. DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17654442

Outlines a falsifiable experimental program across four independent platforms, designed to probe whether physical scaling follows a QDL-governed \(3L + 2F\) ledger.
