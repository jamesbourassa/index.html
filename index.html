Quantized Dimensional Ledger (QDL)

A structural admissibility framework that constrains physical models through dimensional closure, prior to phenomenological fitting. QDL functions as a prediction-filtering and validation layer across physics, cosmology, and measurement.

Selected Applications

The QDL framework has been applied across domains. These applications are methodologically independent but structurally unified by dimensional closure.

Cosmology Closure-restricted alternatives to dark sectors and ΛCDM phenomenology, with correlated expansion–growth signatures.
Field theory & renormalization Operator admissibility, constrained couplings, and discrete RG structure from closure restrictions.
Measurement & metrology Dimensional auditing and pre-verification of measurement models and pipelines for interoperability and trust.
Engineering systems Validation layers for large-scale systems and high-stakes pipelines (including fusion-energy case studies).

Why dimensional closure?

Modern scientific models often satisfy unit consistency while remaining structurally under-constrained. QDL strengthens dimensional analysis into a closure condition that restricts admissible terms, couplings, and normalizations before empirical fitting. The result is not a new physical theory, but a structural filter on the space of allowable models.
