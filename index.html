Scope & limits (read this first):

QDL is not a replacement for quantum field theory, general relativity, or effective field theory. It proposes a structural constraint layer that can be tested through basis-invariance checks, scaling behavior, and closure consistency on declared model families. If empirically successful models systematically violate ledger closure under declared transforms, the closure postulate is falsified.

Intended role: a constraint layer on admissible representations ‚Äî not a claim of new particles, forces, or dynamics.

Quantized Dimensional Ledger (QDL)
Conserved 3L + 2F dimensional cell (proposed)

QDL treats dimensional exponents as entries in a ledger over a two-base space of Length (L) and Frequency (F), with a proposed conserved 3L + 2F Quantized Dimensional Cell used to evaluate admissibility of stated fields, couplings, and constants.

In this picture, the Quantized Dimensional Cell is treated as more than a unit convention: it is proposed as a discrete, conserved dimensional target used to evaluate admissibility. Cross-domain implications (EFT structure, gravitation, constants, metrology) are presented as applications and hypotheses subject to falsifiable checks.

Current application areas under investigation include:

  • EFT/SMEFT operator organization evaluated against explicit closure criteria.
  • Gravity and minimum-length proposals explored as ledger-consistency applications (hypothesis stage).
  • Metrology ledgers for constants and quantum measurement units.
  • Proposed experimental scalings in torsion, NV centers, cavity modes, and metamaterials.

Together, these elements aim to evaluate whether dimensional closure can function as a structural admissibility principle beyond conventional unit bookkeeping.
