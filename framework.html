The Quantized Dimensional Ledger Framework

The Quantized Dimensional Ledger (QDL) is a unified dimensional-closure framework built on a minimal two-basis system, Length (L) and Frequency (F), extended to a 3L + 2F ledger. All fields, couplings, and constants are expressed as integer-exponent vectors in this ledger, closing onto a conserved Quantized Dimensional Cell (QDC) with dimensional content approximately L3F2. This closure principle turns dimensional analysis into a prediction filter for effective field theory (EFT) operator content, gravitational and scalar–tensor structure, the ontology of constants, and precision metrology, tying these sectors into a single structural program with falsifiable experimental predictions.

Key Concepts of QDL

  1. QDL as a prediction filter. QDL is formulated as a global structural filter on admissible field content, EFT operators, and constant combinations. A conserved 3L + 2F cell defines the ledger, and closure rules then determine which operators and dimensional relations are allowed, excluded, or related by integer-lattice structure.
    Details: The Quantized Dimensional Ledger as a Prediction Filter for Field Content, EFT Structure, Constants, Gravity, and Precision Measurement (Zenodo 17848782).
  2. 3L + 2F dimensional basis and unified ledger. QDL replaces the traditional MLT system with a minimal Length–Frequency basis, expanded to a five-slot 3L + 2F ledger, so every quantity is represented as a fixed integer-exponent vector. This supports a global view of all fields and observables on one lattice, across Standard Model, gravity, and metrology sectors.
    Details: Quantized Dimensional Ledger as a Unified Dimensional Closure Framework for the Standard Model and Gravity (17742903) and prediction-filter paper (17848782).
  3. Quantized Dimensional Cell (QDC). The QDC is a conserved “volume element” in the ledger with dimensional structure L3F2. It acts as the closure target: allowed actions and interactions are those whose net ledger content is an integer multiple of the QDC. This gives the ledger a geometric and group-theoretic interpretation.
    Details: The Quantized Dimensional Cell as an SO(3,2) Space–Time Structure (17683786).
  4. Dimensional-closure principle and EFT / SMEFT filtering. Instead of “dimensional consistency” as a loose check, QDL imposes an exact ledger-closure condition: sums of field and coupling exponents must map onto integer multiples of the QDC. This provides a structural filter on admissible Lagrangian terms, operator classes, and dimensional patterns of Wilson coefficients.
    Details: prediction-filter and EFT-lattice papers (17848782, 17773324), and SMEFT application (17780443).
  5. Ledger representation of Standard Model fields and interactions. Gauge fields, matter fields, and couplings are each assigned a 5-component ledger vector. Gauge invariance, anomaly cancellation, and interaction structure are rephrased as integer relations between these vectors under the closure principle, and consistency with the QDC restricts admissible combinations.
    Details: unified Standard Model + gravity framework (17742903) and EFT lattice structure (17773324).
  6. Minimum-length geometry and quantum gravity from dimensional coherence. The gravitational sector is developed so that dimensional coherence under the QDL ledger leads to a minimum-length geometry and coherence criteria for scalar–tensor structure. Gravitation is tied back to the same QDC that governs EFT and constants.
    Details: Quantum Gravity from Dimensional Coherence: Minimum-Length Geometry from the Quantized Dimensional Ledger (17848573); see also scalar–tensor field framework (17704896).
  7. Coherent Ledger Fields and scalar–tensor–EM phenomenology. The QDL gravitation and ledger framework is connected to observable scalar–tensor and electromagnetic phenomenology, including testable predictions for torsion balances, cavity scaling, NV-center resonance, metamaterials, and magnetic-moment structures.
    Details: Coherent Ledger Fields: From SO(3,2) Dimensional Cells to a Unified Scalar–Tensor–Electromagnetic Phenomenology (17803804) and magnetic-moment ledger paper (17693061).
  8. Ledger-based ontology of constants and metrology. Fundamental constants (c, ħ, G, etc.) and metrological chains are re-expressed as ledger vectors and combinations that close onto the QDC, clarifying which constants represent structural geometry versus unit conventions, and providing a practical Quantum Measurement Units (QMU) scheme.
    Details: metrology and constants papers (17619526, 17663436, 17663340), and program overview (17805400).
  9. Experimental validation roadmap. QDL is tied to falsifiable predictions in four main experimental channels: torsion-balance scaling, NV-center frequency shifts, cavity-mode L–F scaling, and metamaterial dispersion-collapse tests, each expressed directly in the ledger and linked back to the same closure rules that govern EFT and gravity.
    Details: QDL Experimental Validation Protocol (17654442) and Teaching dimensional analysis with a length–frequency basis (17663340).
