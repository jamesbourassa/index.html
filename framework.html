Quantized Dimensional Ledger as a Unified Closure Structure

The Quantized Dimensional Ledger (QDL) reformulates physical theories in a minimal dimensional basis of Length (L) and Frequency (F), organized around a conserved 3L + 2F Quantized Dimensional Cell (QDC). All fields, couplings, and constants are expressed as integer exponents on this ledger, providing a single closure rule that spans the Standard Model, scalar–tensor gravitation, and metrology.

1. Ledger basis and Quantized Dimensional Cell

QDL replaces the conventional MLT dimensional basis with a two-base system: Length (L) and Frequency (F). Physical quantities are represented as integer exponent pairs on an L–F ledger, subject to a conserved 3L + 2F Quantized Dimensional Cell. The QDC acts as a discrete dimensional volume element that constrains admissible combinations of fields and couplings.

2. Standard Model sector

Within this ledger, Standard Model fields, gauge couplings, and interaction terms are assigned specific L–F exponents that must collectively respect the 3L + 2F closure. This allows:

  • Gauge-invariant terms to be checked against a single dimensional rule.
  • Triangle anomalies and related consistency conditions to be expressed in ledger form.
  • Mass scales and symmetry-breaking structures to be tracked via their dimensional slots.

3. Scalar–tensor gravitation

On the gravitational side, QDL accommodates a scalar–tensor theory in which the scalar and tensor sectors share the same ledger structure as the Standard Model. The Einstein–Hilbert term, scalar potentials, and coupling terms are all embedded into the 3L + 2F closure rule, tying geometric dynamics and matter content to a common dimensional cell.

4. Physical constants and metrology

The QDL metrology program interprets fundamental constants as structured entries in a ledger of quantum measurement units. Rather than treating constants as separate dimensional tokens, QDL assigns them positions in an L–F exponent table organized by the Quantized Dimensional Cell. This supports:

  • A unified treatment of constants across particle physics, gravity, and electromagnetism.
  • Ledger-based consistency checks between experimental units and theoretical models.
  • Educational tools for teaching dimensional analysis with a length–frequency basis.

5. Relation to geometry and experiments

The QDC is further explored as an SO(3,2) space–time structure, linking the ledger to a specific geometric framework. Experimental tests are proposed in torsion balances, NV-center systems, cavity resonances, and metamaterials to probe whether nature respects the QDL closure scalings.

Role in the QDL corpus.
The unified framework manuscript sets the overall ledger architecture. Companion papers develop the SO(3,2) QDC geometry, the ledger derivation of electron and muon g–2, the metrology and constants ledgers, and the experimental protocol that makes the program testable.
