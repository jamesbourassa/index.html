Framework Overview

Unified Dimensional Closure Framework for the Standard Model and Gravity

The Quantized Dimensional Ledger (QDL) treats dimensional exponents as a conserved ledger across all fields, couplings, and constants, then demands closure of that ledger in a minimal length–frequency basis.

Primary reference:  J. D. Bourassa (2025), “Quantized Dimensional Ledger as a Unified Dimensional Closure Framework for the Standard Model and Gravity”, Zenodo DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17742903.

1. Dimensional closure in a 3L + 2F ledger

QDL replaces the conventional MLT basis with a minimal Length–Frequency basis extended to a 3L + 2F ledger. Every quantity in the theory—fields, couplings, action densities, and constants—is assigned an integer-valued exponent vector in this five-slot ledger.

The core requirement is dimensional closure: allowed terms in the action and allowed combinations of fields must sum to integer multiples of a conserved “dimensional cell”, rather than merely being dimensionally consistent in an informal sense.

Ledger map: each quantity X is assigned a vector ℓ(X) = (ℓ1, ℓ2, ℓ3, ℓ4, ℓ5) in the 3L + 2F basis.

2. The Quantized Dimensional Cell (QDC)

The framework identifies a special conserved unit in ledger space: the Quantized Dimensional Cell (QDC). This cell has dimensional content approximately AQDC ∼ L3F2, and acts as the “closure target” for all allowed combinations of fields and couplings.

In the full construction, the QDC is embedded into a 5D pseudo-orthogonal geometry with an SO(3,2) metric, giving the ledger a geometric interpretation that links dimensional exponents to a discrete space–time–frequency structure.

Closure condition: sums of ledger vectors associated with physical processes must map onto integer multiples of the QDC.

3. Standard Model and gravity in a single ledger

Within this ledger, Standard Model gauge and matter fields are assigned specific 3L + 2F exponent vectors. Gauge invariance, anomaly cancellation, and interaction structure are all reformulated as integer relations between these vectors subject to QDC closure.

The gravitational sector is realised as a scalar–tensor theory whose fields and couplings live in the same ledger. The dimensional-closure requirement ties cosmological terms and local terms back into the QDC, giving a unified structural filter on admissible actions that span both the Standard Model and gravitation.

4. Metrology and the ontology of constants

By forcing all constants to live in the same ledger as fields and actions, QDL clarifies which constants encode structural geometry and which encode unit conventions. The program provides a Quantum Measurement Units (QMU) scheme grounded in the QDC and in integer-exponent combinations of standard constants.

This links metrology directly to the ledger, so that the choice of standards and the construction of experimental chains can be analysed in the same 3L + 2F space as the underlying theoretical fields.
