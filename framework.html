The Quantized Dimensional Ledger Framework

The Quantized Dimensional Ledger (QDL) is a unified dimensional-closure framework built on a minimal two-basis system, Length (L) and Frequency (F), extended to a 3L + 2F ledger. All fields, couplings, and constants are expressed as integer-exponent vectors in this ledger, closing onto a conserved Quantized Dimensional Cell (QDC) with dimensional content approximately L3F2. This closure principle reorganizes the Standard Model, gravitation, and metrology into a single structural program with falsifiable experimental predictions.

Key Concepts of QDL

  1. 3L + 2F dimensional basis. QDL replaces the traditional MLT system with a minimal Length–Frequency basis, expanded to a five-slot ledger (3L + 2F), so every quantity is represented as a fixed integer-exponent vector. This supports a global view of all fields and observables on one lattice.
    Details: Quantized Dimensional Ledger as a Unified Dimensional Closure Framework for the Standard Model and Gravity (Zenodo 17742903).
  2. Quantized Dimensional Cell (QDC). The QDC is a conserved “volume element” in the ledger with dimensional structure L3F2. It acts as the closure target: allowed actions and interactions are those whose net ledger content is an integer multiple of the QDC.
    Details: The Quantized Dimensional Cell as an SO(3,2) Space–Time Structure (Zenodo 17683786).
  3. Dimensional-closure principle. Instead of “dimensional consistency” as a loose check, QDL imposes an exact ledger-closure condition: sums of field and coupling exponents must map onto integer multiples of the QDC. This provides a structural filter on admissible Lagrangian terms and interaction vertices.
    Details: unified framework and metrology/constant papers (17742903, 17619526, 17663436).
  4. Ledger representation of Standard Model fields. Gauge fields, matter fields, and couplings are each assigned a 5-component ledger vector. Gauge invariance, anomaly cancellation, and interaction structure are rephrased as integer relations between these vectors under the closure principle.
    Details: unified Standard Model + gravity framework (17742903).
  5. Scalar–tensor gravitation in QDL. The gravitational sector is built as a scalar–tensor theory whose fields and couplings live in the QDL ledger, enforcing dimensional closure across cosmological and local terms and tying the gravitation sector back into the same QDC structure as the Standard Model.
    Details: A Unified Ledger-Based Framework for Physical Theories: From Dimensional Cells to Scalar–Tensor Field Structure (17704896).
  6. Ledger-based ontology of constants and metrology. Fundamental constants (c, ħ, G, etc.) and metrological chains are re-expressed as ledger vectors and combinations that close onto the QDC, clarifying which constants represent structural geometry versus unit conventions, and providing a practical Quantum Measurement Units (QMU) scheme.
    Details: metrology and constants papers (17619526, 17663436, 17663340).
  7. Experimental validation roadmap. QDL is tied to falsifiable predictions in four main experimental channels: torsion-balance scaling, NV-center frequency shifts, cavity-mode L–F scaling, and metamaterial dispersion-collapse tests, each expressed directly in the ledger.
    Details: QDL Experimental Validation Protocol (17654442) and Teaching dimensional analysis with a length–frequency basis (17663340).
