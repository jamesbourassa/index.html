Framework Overview

Unified Dimensional Closure Framework for the Standard Model and Gravity

The Quantized Dimensional Ledger (QDL) treats dimensional exponents as a conserved ledger across all fields, couplings, and constants, then demands closure of that ledger in a minimal length–frequency basis.

Primary reference:  J. D. Bourassa (2025), “Quantized Dimensional Ledger as a Unified Dimensional Closure Framework for the Standard Model and Gravity”, Zenodo DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17742903.

In conventional dimensional analysis, units are bookkeeping. In QDL, the ledger itself becomes a geometric and algebraic constraint on admissible physical theories. The unified framework paper is the canonical statement of this idea.

1. Ledger Basis and Dimensional Cell

The framework adopts a two-generator dimensional basis built on length and frequency. All physical quantities are expressed as integer exponents in this ledger, and the combination of exponents defines a Quantized Dimensional Cell that acts as a conserved volume element in the ledger space.

The dimensional cell plays a dual role: it encodes conservation of dimensional structure and serves as the organizing unit for embedding fields and couplings.

2. Standard Model Sector

Gauge fields, matter fields, and interaction terms from the Standard Model are assigned QDL ledger exponents. The requirement of dimensional closure constrains how these exponents can combine, leading to:

  • consistent ledger assignments for gauge and matter fields,
  • ledger-compatible interaction terms,
  • relationships among scales and couplings that maintain closure.

The goal is not to rewrite the Standard Model from scratch, but to show that its field content and couplings can be understood as a ledger-consistent configuration within a closed dimensional structure.

3. Scalar–Tensor Gravitational Sector

The framework supplements the Standard Model embedding with a scalar–tensor description of gravitation. The scalar and tensor components are treated as ledger fields with specific dimensional exponents, and the closure condition restricts admissible terms in the gravitational action.

This leads to a scalar–tensor sector that is not arbitrary, but shaped by the same dimensional-closure principles that govern the Standard Model fields.

4. Constants and Metrology

Fundamental constants are recast as ledger objects rather than primitive inputs. Their exponents in the L–F basis are constrained by the requirement that all physical laws draw from a closed set of ledger cells. This perspective links metrology directly to theory structure and supports a unified ontology of constants.

5. Relation to Geometry and Precision Tests

The unified framework paper is complemented by separate works that:

  • identify the Quantized Dimensional Cell with an SO(3,2) space–time structure,
  • derive electron and muon g–2 factors from ledger geometry,
  • and propose experimental protocols for torsion, NV-centers, cavity scaling, and metamaterials.

Together, these works extend the ledger from abstract dimensional constraints to concrete geometric interpretation and precision observables.

6. Role in the Broader QDL Program

Within the QDL corpus, the unified dimensional closure framework paper serves as:

  • the primary reference for the L–F ledger and closure principle,
  • the organizing hub for Standard Model and gravitational sectors,
  • and the conceptual anchor for experimental validation efforts.

Subsequent manuscripts refer back to this framework as the starting point for more specialized geometric, metrological, or phenomenological investigations.
