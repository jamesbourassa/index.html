QDL Physics Institute

The QDL Physics Institute is an independent research program based in Huntley, Illinois, USA. Its purpose is to develop, formalize, and experimentally test the Quantized Dimensional Ledger (QDL) as a unified framework for physical theories and precision metrology.

Mission

The Institute’s mission is to:

  • Establish QDL as a rigorous, mathematically coherent dimensional closure framework.
  • Embed the Standard Model, gravitation, and metrology into a common 3L + 2F ledger.
  • Design and disseminate falsifiable experimental tests of QDL predictions.
  • Make all derivations, tables, and protocols publicly accessible and auditable.

Research themes

  • Dimensional analysis and ledger geometry in an L–F basis.
  • Scalar–tensor gravitation and geometric structures linked to the Quantized Dimensional Cell.
  • Ledger-based treatments of fundamental constants and quantum measurement units.
  • Precision tabletop experiments in torsion balances, NV centers, cavities, and metamaterials.
  • Educational materials for teaching dimensional analysis using QDL concepts.

Location and contact

Location: Huntley, Illinois, USA
Contact: via the dedicated contact page, which includes email and a simple contact form for journal editors, experimental collaborators, and students.

Collaboration interests

The Institute welcomes constructive engagement from:

  • Journal editors and referees in gravitation, mathematical physics, and metrology.
  • Experimental groups working on torsion balances, NV centers, cavity resonators, or metamaterials.
  • Researchers interested in dimensional analysis, units systems, and geometric foundations of physics.
  • Educators seeking to incorporate length–frequency dimensional analysis into teaching materials.

For collaboration inquiries, please use the contact page and briefly describe your research context and interest in QDL.
