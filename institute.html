QDL Physics Institute

The QDL Physics Institute is an independent research program based in Huntley, Illinois, USA. Its purpose is to develop, formalize, and test the Quantized Dimensional Ledger (QDL) as a dimensional-closure and model-admissibility framework. QDL functions as a prediction-filter and validation layer that constrains admissible field content, couplings, operators, and measurement relations prior to phenomenological fitting, anchored on a conserved 3L + 2F Quantized Dimensional Cell (QDC).

Mission

The Institute’s mission is to:

  • Establish QDL as a rigorous dimensional-closure and admissibility framework for physical theories and measurement pipelines.
  • Provide a framework-first validation layer that precedes phenomenology, data fitting, or numerical modeling.
  • Embed the Standard Model, gravitation, EFT/SMEFT, and metrology into a shared 3L + 2F ledger closing onto a conserved QDC.
  • Develop explicit, falsifiable experimental tests of QDL predictions across torsion balances, NV centers, cavity resonators, and metamaterials.
  • Maintain full transparency via open-access publications, ledger tables, and experimental protocols.
Research themes
  • Dimensional analysis as admissibility, reconstructed via a length–frequency ledger and Quantized Dimensional Cell.
  • Prediction-filter methods constraining EFT, SMEFT, gravity, and constant structure before fitting.
  • Technical rigor through ledger-constrained renormalization and operator pruning.
  • Real-world applications in metrology, engineering models, and measurement-pipeline auditing.
  • Falsifiable tabletop experiments probing QDL-driven scaling laws and coherence criteria.

Program structure & canonical references

The Institute’s work follows a framework-first ordering reflected across the website.

The QDL program is organized around a small set of canonical papers that define the framework, its methodological role, technical rigor, and real-world applicability:

  • Dimensional Closure as a National-Scale Model Validation Layer — canonical framework definition (Zenodo: 10.5281/zenodo.17979789).
  • Structural Reconstruction of Dimensional Analysis — foundational reformulation of dimensional reasoning (10.5281/zenodo.17882709).
  • QDL as a Prediction Filter — admissibility constraints across EFT, gravity, constants, and metrology (10.5281/zenodo.17848782).
  • Ledger-Constrained Renormalization — technical rigor via operator pruning and discrete RG structure (10.5281/zenodo.18025072).
  • Dimensional-Closure Auditing — real-world application to engineering models and measurement pipelines (10.5281/zenodo.18025343).

Domain-specific applications (cosmology, unified SM + gravity, coherent ledger fields, SMEFT case studies, and experimental protocols) are developed downstream of this core structure.
