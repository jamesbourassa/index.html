The QDL Physics Institute

The QDL Physics Institute is an independent research initiative based in Huntley, Illinois, dedicated to the Quantized Dimensional Ledger (QDL): a unified dimensional framework for gravitation, electromagnetism, metrology, and physical constants.

Mission & Focus

A small, focused program aimed at building a transparent, falsifiable dimensional foundation for modern physics.

Mission

The Institute develops the Quantized Dimensional Ledger and Quantized Dimensional Cell (QDC) as organizing principles for field theory, gravitation, and metrology. The emphasis is on:

  • Formal dimensional closure and ledger-based field structures
  • Transparent derivations with auditable ledgers and tables
  • Concrete, falsifiable predictions tied to tabletop experiments
  • Open dissemination of manuscripts, data, and teaching materials

Research Themes

  • 3L + 2F ledger formulation of physical quantities
  • QDC as an SO(3,2)-like space–time structure
  • Scalar–tensor gravitation constrained by QDL closure
  • Ledger geometry of magnetic moments and g–2
  • QMU ledgers for physical constants and metrology
  • Experimental protocols: torsion, NV centers, cavities, metamaterials
