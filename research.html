The Quantized Dimensional Ledger for Metrology: Dimensional Closure, QMU Ledgers, and the Ontology of Physical Constants

Intended venue: Journal of Theoretical and Applied Physics (JTAP) · 2025

Introduces the 3L + 2F dimensional basis and a 100-entry Quantum Measurement Unit (QMU) ledger spanning mechanics, electromagnetism, gravitation, and thermodynamics, with a focus on dimensional closure and the classification of physical constants.

3L + 2F dimensional basis QMU ledger (100 entries) Fundamental constants SI & CODATA structure

The paper develops a dimensional ontology rather than new dynamics, classifying constants into dimensionless invariants, dimensional couplings with fixed exponents, and emergent geometric–frequency ratios. Quantitative ledger-based demonstrations compare the structural dependence of h, R∞, and Z0 against CODATA values and modern SI definitions.

Pedagogical manuscript

Teaching Dimensional Analysis with a Length–Frequency Basis: An Inverse-Length Cavity Scaling Lab

Physics education / American Journal of Physics-style manuscript · 2025

Explores a reduced {L, F} dimensional basis for wave-based systems and presents a simple resonant-cavity lab where the dominant mode obeys F ∝ 1/L, providing a clean, falsifiable scaling test that surfaces ledger structure without altering underlying physics.

Dimensional analysis Length–frequency basis Resonant cavity lab Physics education

The manuscript frames the {L, F} ledger as a teaching tool rather than a replacement for standard SI primitives. Students measure resonance frequencies as a function of cavity length, extract the log–log slope, and compare against the predicted exponent n = −1, illustrating how dimensional closure appears in a concrete, low-cost laboratory setting.
