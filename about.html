Research Profile

Dimensional ontology, quantum metrology, and unified frameworks for physical constants.

Overview

Bourassa’s current work develops the Quantized Dimensional Ledger (QDL), a unified 3L + 2F length–frequency basis intended to bring algebraic closure and conceptual clarity to mechanical, electromagnetic, gravitational, and thermodynamic quantities. Within this framework, mass and time are treated as derived constructs, while the Quantum Measurement Unit (QMU) provides a common dimensional reference cell.

His research focuses on:

  • Constructing a 100-entry QMU ledger with cross-domain dimensional closure.
  • Classifying fundamental constants into invariants, couplings, and emergent ratios.
  • Exploring scalar–tensor extensions of Einstein–Maxwell theory aligned with QDL.
  • Designing laboratory-scale tests (torsion balances, NV centers) sensitive to QDL effects.

Canonical QDL Papers

Core references for the Quantized Dimensional Ledger research program.

The Quantized Dimensional Ledger for Metrology: Dimensional Closure, QMU Ledgers, and the Ontology of Physical Constants

Bourassa, J. D. (2025). Zenodo. DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17619526

Establishes the QDL framework as a unified 3L + 2F dimensional basis and presents a 100-entry Quantum Measurement Unit (QMU) ledger spanning mechanics, electromagnetism, gravitation, and thermodynamics. The work develops a dimensional ontology for physical constants, separating them into dimensionless invariants, dimensional couplings, and emergent geometric–frequency ratios. The Zenodo record includes the full ledger in CSV format and documentation suitable for independent audit and reuse.

A Quantized Dimensional Ledger Framework for Gravitation, Electromagnetism, and Metrology

Bourassa, J. D. (2025). Zenodo. DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17515009

Earlier, broader preprint outlining the QDL framework as a unifying dimensional scaffold for gravitation, electromagnetism, and precision measurement. This manuscript provides the conceptual bridge from traditional dimensional analysis to a ledger-based treatment of physical quantities and motivates the more refined metrology-focused work in the later QDL Metrology paper.

For Journal Editors & Collaborators

Notes on scope, reproducibility, and how the QDL work fits within theoretical and applied physics.

Scope & Reproducibility

The QDL program is intended as a dimensional ontology and metrological framework rather than a competing dynamical theory. The emphasis is on:

  • Algebraic closure across mechanical, electromagnetic, and gravitational quantities.
  • Ledger-style documentation of exponents and units for transparent audit.
  • Clear separation of dimensional structure from specific field equations.

All ledger data supporting recent manuscripts are publicly available via Zenodo, with machine-readable CSV files and explanatory README documentation to facilitate independent analysis, replication, and critical review.
