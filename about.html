About the QDL Physics Institute

The QDL Physics Institute is dedicated to developing and testing the Quantized Dimensional Ledger (QDL) as a unifying framework for modern physics. The core idea is to recast physical theories in a minimal two-basis dimensional system built on length and frequency, then require a strict dimensional-closure condition across all fields, couplings, and constants.

The flagship paper, “Quantized Dimensional Ledger as a Unified Dimensional Closure Framework for the Standard Model and Gravity” (J. D. Bourassa, 2025, Zenodo DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17742903 ), provides the current reference formulation of the unified QDL framework.

Research Program

The research program proceeds along four tightly linked axes:

  • Unified dimensional framework: Construction of a closed L–F ledger that embeds the Standard Model, scalar–tensor gravitation, and physical constants into a single coherent structure.
  • Geometric foundations: Interpreting the Quantized Dimensional Cell (QDC) as an SO(3,2) space–time element and exploring its implications for field representations.
  • Precision observables: Deriving testable quantities such as electron and muon g–2 from ledger geometry, and connecting QDL predictions to high-precision data.
  • Experimental validation: Designing falsifiable tabletop experiments involving torsion balances, NV-centers, cavity scaling, and metamaterial dispersion.
