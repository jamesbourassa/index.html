Research Profile

Overview

Bourassa’s current work develops the Quantized Dimensional Ledger (QDL), a unified 3L + 2F length–frequency ledger in which every field, coupling, and constant is assigned an integer-exponent vector and must close onto a conserved Quantized Dimensional Cell. In this framework, dimensional analysis becomes a prediction filter for admissible EFT operators, gravitational actions, and constant combinations, rather than a bookkeeping exercise.

The program now spans:

  • A capstone QDL prediction filter for field content, EFT structure, gravity, and constants.
  • Quantum gravity from dimensional coherence and minimum-length geometry.
  • Unified Standard Model + gravity closure on a shared ledger.
  • Ledger-closure constraints on the SMEFT operator basis and Wilson coefficients.
  • Coherent ledger fields connecting SO(3,2) QDC geometry to scalar–tensor–EM phenomenology.
  • Metrology-ledger and ontology-of-constants work via Quantum Measurement Units (QMU).
  • An experimental roadmap across torsion balances, NV centers, cavity scaling, and metamaterials.

Flagship QDL Papers

Core references for understanding the structure, scope, and testable claims of the QDL program.

The Quantized Dimensional Ledger as a Prediction Filter for Field Content, EFT Structure, Constants, Gravity, and Precision Measurement

Bourassa, J. D. (2025). Zenodo. DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17848782

Capstone formulation of QDL as a structural prediction filter on admissible field content, EFT operators, and constant combinations. A conserved 3L + 2F cell defines the ledger, and closure rules link EFT structure, gravitational sectors, and precision-metrology scalings within a single quantized dimensional framework.

Quantum Gravity from Dimensional Coherence: Minimum-Length Geometry from the Quantized Dimensional Ledger

Bourassa, J. D. (2025). Zenodo. DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17848573

Develops a gravitational sector built to respect QDL dimensional coherence, yielding a minimum-length geometry and scalar–tensor criteria tied back to the same QDC that constrains EFT and constants. Positions QDL as a bridge between dimensional ontology and quantum-gravity structure.

Quantized Dimensional Ledger as a Unified Dimensional Closure Framework for the Standard Model and Gravity

Bourassa, J. D. (2025). Zenodo. DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17742903

Provides a unified closure framework in which Standard Model fields and scalar–tensor gravity share a common ledger representation and are subject to the same QDC-based dimensional rules. Serves as the main bridge between particle-physics content and the quantum-gravity and prediction-filter papers.

Coherent Ledger Fields: From SO(3,2) Dimensional Cells to a Unified Scalar–Tensor–Electromagnetic Phenomenology

Bourassa, J. D. (2025). Zenodo. DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17803804

Connects the SO(3,2)-like QDC geometry and ledger structure to scalar–tensor–EM phenomenology, including proposed signatures in torsion-balance experiments, NV-center frequency shifts, cavity-mode scaling, metamaterials, and magnetic-moment structure.

Ledger-Closure Constraints on the SMEFT: A Lattice-Theoretic Derivation of Operator Exclusions and Wilson-Coefficient Relations

Bourassa, J. D. (2025). Zenodo. DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17780443

Applies QDL ledger-closure rules to the Standard Model Effective Field Theory, deriving structured operator exclusions and dimensional relations among Wilson coefficients. Uses the EFT-lattice representation and prediction-filter structure as a basis for classifying viable SMEFT operators.

The Quantized Dimensional Ledger for Metrology: Dimensional Closure, QMU Ledgers, and the Ontology of Physical Constants

Bourassa, J. D. (2025). Zenodo. DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17619526

Develops QDL as a metrology framework via the Quantum Measurement Unit (QMU) ledger, with a 100-entry dimensional ledger spanning mechanics, electromagnetism, gravity, and thermodynamics, and an explicit ontology for physical constants.

For Journal Editors & Collaborators

Notes on scope, reproducibility, and how the QDL program fits within theoretical, metrological, and experimental physics.

Scope & Reproducibility

The QDL program is framed as a dimensional-closure and prediction-filter architecture, not a replacement for established field equations. The emphasis is on:

  • Algebraic closure across mechanical, electromagnetic, gravitational, and EFT quantities.
  • Ledger-style documentation of exponents and units for transparent audit.
  • Explicit separation of dimensional structure from specific dynamical choices.
  • Public availability of ledger tables, metrology datasets, and supplemental derivations.

All central manuscripts are accompanied by Zenodo records with machine-readable CSV tables and explanatory notes, enabling independent verification, critique, and reuse.
