About the QDL Physics Institute and James D. Bourassa

The QDL Physics Institute is led by James D. Bourassa, an independent researcher focused on dimensional analysis, metrology, and foundational physics. The Institute develops the Quantized Dimensional Ledger (QDL) as a unified 3L + 2F length–frequency basis for physical theories, with an emphasis on clear derivations, reproducibility, and experimental testability.

Research focus

The current program centers on:

  • Formulating QDL as a dimensional closure framework spanning the Standard Model and gravitation.
  • Interpreting the Quantized Dimensional Cell as a geometric structure (SO(3,2) space–time).
  • Developing ledger-based treatments of electron and muon g–2 and other precision observables.
  • Building metrology ledgers for physical constants and quantum measurement units.
  • Designing falsifiable tabletop experiments to probe QDL scaling predictions.
