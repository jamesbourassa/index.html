Scientific Background

For journal editors, reviewers, and collaborators seeking context on the QDL research program.

James D. Bourassa is the founder and director of the QDL Physics Institute, where he leads the development of the Quantized Dimensional Ledger (QDL)—a unified physical framework grounded in dimensional closure, geometric consistency, and predictive experimental design.

His current research advances the Quantum Measurement Unit (QMU) audit into a comprehensive theoretical system that integrates:

  • a covariant field-theoretic Lagrangian,
  • 3 + 2 dimensional (length–frequency) metric geometry,
  • scalar–tensor extensions of Einstein–Maxwell theory,
  • and cross-domain retrodictions linking atomic, electromagnetic, gravitational, and cosmological regimes.

Bourassa’s recent work introduces geometric dynamics built on variational principles with field symmetries under SO(3,2), enabling a coherent mapping between dimensional structure and physical ontology. Within this construction, the legacy Aether Unit is reinterpreted as a quantized SO(3,2) space–time fiber, providing a mathematically transparent foundation for the QDL’s unified treatment of constants, fields, and interactions.

His research program emphasizes mathematically rigorous ledger-style dimensional closure, parameter-free or minimally parameterized predictions, and falsifiable laboratory experiments spanning torsion balances, NV centers, electromagnetic stress modulation, and precision metrology.

Through the QDL Physics Institute, Bourassa is developing a modern, experimentally grounded approach to unification that connects geometry, electromagnetism, gravitation, and measurement science within a single dimensional and variational framework.
