Experimental Roadmap

QDL Experimental Validation Program

The QDL Experimental Validation Protocol proposes four independent tabletop platforms— torsion balances, NV centers, cavity scaling, and metamaterials—to test how a \(3L + 2F\) Quantized Dimensional Cell constrains physical scaling laws.

Platforms

Each platform targets a different corner of the QDL framework, with emphasis on falsifiable predictions and practical laboratory implementation.

Torsion-Balance Scaling

Gravitational / mechanical sector

Precision torsion-balance experiments probe whether torque and deflection follow QDL-predicted scaling when arm length, mass distribution, and environmental parameters are systematically varied in a \(3L + 2F\) ledger.

NV-Center Frequency Shifts

Spin and quantum-defect sector

Nitrogen–vacancy centers in diamond offer narrow spin resonances. QDL-motivated field configurations are used to search for small, structured frequency offsets that cannot be accounted for by standard Zeeman or crystal-field models alone.

Cavity L–F Scaling

Optical / microwave resonators

Tunable cavities provide a direct way to test QDL’s length–frequency basis. Mode structure and resonance scaling are compared against standard EM expectations and the integer-exponent ledger implied by the Quantized Dimensional Cell.

Metamaterial Dispersion

Engineered media and dispersion

Metamaterial lattices are designed to approximate QDC-like coherence. Their dispersion, resonance collapse, and anomalous modes are analyzed through the QDL ledger to search for signatures of additional structured degrees of freedom.

For full details, proposed parameter ranges, and suggested analysis pipelines, see: Bourassa, J. (2025). QDL Experimental Validation Protocol: A Unified Roadmap for Torsion, NV-Center, Cavity, and Metamaterial Tests. Zenodo. DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17654442.
